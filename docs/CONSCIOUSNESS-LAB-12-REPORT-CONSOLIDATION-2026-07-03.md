## Corpus Consensus

The reports converge on six hard positions.

1. **Butlin 14 is the formal rubric.** It is a theory-derived indicator framework, not a proof device.
2. **Thaler 9 is an engineering reconstruction.** It is useful and already implemented locally, but it should
   be named as a reconstructed Thaler/Creativity-Machine lens rather than a community-standard checklist.
3. **Parameter count is the wrong primitive.** Architecture, recurrence, perturbability, embodiment,
   self-modeling, multiscale dynamics, and causal load-bearingness are the defensible primitives.
4. **The lab must be adversarial.** A public claim is credible only if it has nulls, ablations, preregistered
   predictions, and negative-result preservation.
5. **A singularity moment is a phase transition, not a badge.** It is a replayable convergence across several
   theory families under intervention.
6. **The GitHub Page must lead with science, not creatures.** The order should be theory registry, wiring
   ledger, assays, telemetry, falsifiers, then entity classes.

## Singularity Moment Standard From The Reports

A candidate moment is report-worthy only if it is a compound event:

1. Perturbation complexity rises.
2. Workspace/CTM broadcast becomes selectively occupied.
3. Metacognitive calibration improves under uncertainty.
4. Sensorimotor or active-inference control improves actual downstream behavior.
5. Dynamics remain metastable or critical-like without tipping into random noise.
6. At least one causal integration or field/partition metric changes in the predicted direction.
7. The event repeats from seed and weakens under targeted ablation.
8. A matched null baseline fails to reproduce the same joint transition.

The label remains: **consciousness-indicator convergence**, not proof of subjective experience.

The 12 reports do not tell us to claim consciousness harder. They tell us to make the lab harder to fool.

The project should pursue sentience as a direction while writing every public and code-facing claim as an
instrumented, falsifiable proxy. The real leap is not a humanoid performance. It is a small non-human system
whose internal organization survives perturbation, explains behavior, separates from nulls, fails honestly
under ablation, and produces cross-theory convergence that can be replayed from a seed.

That is strange enough to matter and strict enough to defend.
