1. Getting started

Open the dome at /. The simulation runs in your browser via Bun + WebGL. Every run is seeded and deterministic: the same seed reproduces the same population trajectory. Reloading the page starts a fresh session for super-creature evolution, levels, and economy — nothing carries over from the last visit.

First five minutes

  • Watch entities move, trade, split, and mutate in the arena.
  • Open Telemetry (left) for population and chaos readouts.
  • Try Sorting Fields — pick a field or hit AUTO to cycle every 2.5 seconds.
  • Tap dock buttons: AI, HELP, NEURAL, ARCHITECT.
  • Use ⛓ ACCESS when ready to unlock the playable super creature twin.

First 30 minutes

  • Open ⚙ SETTINGS to access all simulation controls: pause, speed, split, burst, mutate, chaos, audio, render mode, view, space, weather, reset, apocalypse, singularity, NHI launch, chaos mode, and entropy.
  • Press ⊞ NEURAL inside the Super Creature panel to open the 4-tab observatory (WORLD, COGNITION, QUANTUM, BRAIN).
  • In the BRAIN tab, click ⬡ MEGA 4D to toggle the 4D tesseract connectome with 420 neurons across 5 cortical layers.
  • Open ⟁ ARCHITECTURE to browse 101 super creatures and their breeding rites. The ⬡ MEGA 4D mode shows the apex brain live.
  • Visit Lab for 36 WildBeyond tiles — strange attractors, reaction-diffusion, and quantum collapse fields.
  • Visit Sentience for headless seed sweeps, null controls, ablations, and exportable consciousness-indicator telemetry.

6. Sorting fields

Twenty-five algorithms sort entities by different keys (energy, age, hue, etc.). Modes:

  • Single — one field at a time; click rows to switch.
  • ALL — every field active; progress bars pulse together.
  • AUTO — advances to the next field every 6 sim-seconds; readout shows countdown. This is a real batched sort — each field runs its own comparator over the live entity array and the progress bar tracks the sort pass.

9. Philosophy & vision

What this is

The Cosmogonic Quantum Mechalogodrom is a solo indie A-Life research dome — a browser-native deterministic simulation of up to 50,000 organisms with emergent intelligence, economy, and culture. Its seeded simulation core is not an LLM or chatbot; the optional Copilot is a read-only shell fenced out of deterministic state. The core is a closed-form, seeded, reproducible artificial life environment.

What it is not

  • Not sentient — the mind models are functional correlates, not phenomenal consciousness.
  • The seeded core performs no language-model inference; optional Copilot text generation is structurally fenced outside the simulation.
  • Not a game in the traditional sense — it is a research instrument you can watch and influence.

Determinism as a design principle

Every random draw in the simulation uses a seeded Rng (mulberry32). No Math.random exists in sim logic. The same seed produces the same population trajectory byte-for-byte. This is essential for reproducibility and scientific defensibility.

Tsotchke quantum corpus

The Tsotchke corpus is a mixed, ledgered integration: 8 deep, 7 wired, 2 harvest, 4 fenced, and 1 metadata repository; 17 of 21 non-meta repositories affect runtime behavior. Direct ports, deterministic adaptations/facades, and inert provenance fences are distinguished explicitly rather than described as blanket gate-for-gate parity. Quantum paths run as deterministic classical statevector or related simulations, not on a physical QPU.

Inspirations

  • Stephen Thaler's Creativity Machine (generator + perceptor)
  • Karl Friston's Free Energy Principle / Active Inference
  • Global Workspace Theory (Baars / Dehaene)
  • Integrated Information Theory (Tononi — Φ)
  • ALife / digital biologics tradition (Ray, Holland, Yaeger)
  • Quantum computing gate-model formalism (Nielsen & Chuang)
  • Strange attractors (Lorenz, Rössler, Thomas)
  • Gray–Scott reaction–diffusion systems
